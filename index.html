History

Quantum computing started with theoretical discussions in the 1980s by physicists like Richard Feynman and David Deutsch. It aims to use the principles of quantum mechanics to perform calculations that classical computers can't handle efficiently.

Algorithm

Quantum algorithms like Shor’s (for factoring large numbers) and Grover’s (for searching unsorted databases) demonstrate exponential or quadratic speedups over classical counterparts. These use quantum principles like superposition and entanglement.

Info

Quantum computing is an emerging field that can transform cryptography, materials science, and complex problem solving. Major companies and universities are actively developing quantum processors and quantum software frameworks.
